Why Low-Impact Doesn’t Mean Low Results

By Sara Spinosa  ·  May 2026  ·  5 min read

One of the biggest misconceptions I hear from new clients is this: “I need to be doing HIIT or heavy lifting to see real results.” I’m here to tell you — that’s simply not true.

Low-impact training — think Pilates-style movements, dumbbell resistance work, ankle-weight exercises, and controlled bodyweight sequences — can build serious strength, improve your posture, increase your muscle tone, and help you lose weight. The difference is that it does all of this without beating up your joints.

What “Low-Impact” Actually Means

Low-impact means at least one foot stays on the ground at all times, or movements are controlled enough that there’s no jarring force on your knees, hips, or spine. It does not mean easy. Some of the hardest workouts I’ve ever done — and put my clients through — are low-impact.

Who It’s Perfect For

  • Anyone with knee, hip, or back sensitivity
  • Seniors wanting to stay active safely
  • People returning to fitness after a long break
  • Anyone who wants to move consistently without constant soreness
  • Honestly — almost everyone

The Results Speak for Themselves

My client Kim came to me at 70 wanting to get stronger and improve her balance. Using a completely low-impact program, she lost 45 lbs over a year and a half and now genuinely looks forward to working out. My client Brittany went from skipping the gym constantly to working out consistently for over a year — same approach.

Consistency beats intensity every single time. And low-impact training is something you can actually keep doing — for years, for decades, for life.
